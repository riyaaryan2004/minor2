# MODEL PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

• Two experiments were conducted to predict mood and productivity using Fitbit activity and physiological data.

• In the first experiment, all features were used and the models achieved very high prediction accuracy for both mood and productivity.

• CatBoost performed best overall among the models, particularly for productivity prediction.

• In the second experiment, feature reduction was applied using only TotalSteps and sleep_hours.

• Mood prediction remained highly accurate even with reduced features, indicating that sleep and physical activity strongly influence mood.

• Productivity prediction accuracy decreased significantly after feature reduction.

• This suggests that productivity depends on additional factors such as activity intensity, sedentary behavior, and heart rate patterns.

• Overall, mood can be predicted using minimal wearable data, while productivity prediction requires a richer set of features.
